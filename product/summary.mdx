## What ORISO Is, in One Paragraph

ORISO is a **multi-tenant, anonymous online counseling platform** built on Kubernetes (k3s + Helm). Clients in distress reach a counselor via a public **live-chat link** or a German-postal-zip-code lookup, enter an **anonymous waiting room** where they get a server-generated pseudonym, sign **two GDPR consents** (platform-default at entry, counseling-center-specific when a counselor accepts their ticket), and chat in an **end-to-end encrypted Matrix room** that is **auto-purged after 48 h**. The **client identity is wiped** the moment they disconnect.

## The Hierarchy in One Sentence

**Platform Admin** owns the platform → creates **Tenants**; each Tenant Admin signs a Data Policy Agreement and creates **Counseling Centers**; each Counseling Center has a **Counselor Admin** who manages **Counselors**; **Clients** are anonymous and ephemeral.

## The Five Things You Must Remember

1. **Anonymity is sacred.** No real names, no IPs persisted, pseudonyms only.
2. **GDPR is taken twice** — once at the waiting-room entry (platform default), once at counselor pickup (center-specific).
3. **Inheritance for legal text** — every counseling center inherits Imprint/Data-Policy/GDPR from its tenant; every tenant inherits from the platform; overrides are local-only.
4. **Wipe-on-disconnect, not archive.** A client who leaves disappears from every store within seconds-to-minutes.
5. **End-to-end encryption** in chat (Megolm) means even the server cannot read message bodies.

## The Tech Stack in One Bullet List

- **Frontend** — React 18 + Vite 4 (`ORISO-Frontend` for clients, `ORISO-Admin` for staff, `ORISO-Element` for chat UI).
- **Identity** — Keycloak OIDC, 14 realm roles, MFA mandatory for Tenant/Platform admins.
- **Backend** — 4 Spring Boot 2.7 services (`TenantService`, `UserService`, `ConsultingTypeService`, `AgencyService`), Java 17, Maven.
- **Communication** — Matrix Synapse (E2EE chat), LiveKit (WebRTC video, no recording).
- **Data** — MariaDB (relational truth), MongoDB (topic configs), PostgreSQL (Synapse), Redis (caches/queue), RabbitMQ (async cleanup).
- **Infra** — k3s + Helm umbrella chart `oriso-platform`, Nginx Ingress + Cert-Manager TLS, SignOZ observability.

## The Core Flow in 6 Steps

1. **Client opens a live-chat link** — `https://app.oriso-dev.site/live-chat/{agency}?topic=debt`.
2. **System creates an anonymous user**, issues a **pseudonym** (e.g. *geschmeidiges Kanninchen Kim*), shows the platform-default GDPR popup.
3. **Client enters the waiting room** — sees queue position ("23 Noch vor Ihnen"), a breathing-game distraction, the "messages auto-deleted in 48 h" promise.
4. **A counselor picks the ticket** — oldest-first, intentionally not auto-assigned. Pickup creates an encrypted Matrix room.
5. **Client signs the counseling-center GDPR**. Without this, the chat **does not open**.
6. **Chat happens, then ends**, room is tombstoned and purged within 48 h. Client pseudonym is wiped on disconnect.

## What Is Not Built Yet (Roadmap)

- **AI-assisted case notes** — designed (browser-side decryption + PII scrubbing + self-hosted summarizer); not implemented.
- **Trainee function** on counselors.
- **Merged zip-code + topic** in a single live-chat link.
- **Simplified link-generation UI** (current is overengineered).
- **Counseling-center deactivated state** instead of hiding live-chat off.
- **Browser-language default with English fallback** (current defaults to German).

## Hard Rules (Non-Negotiable)

- ❌ No `dev` mode in production.
- ❌ No client IPs persisted.
- ❌ No message bodies in any backend log.
- ❌ No archive of an anonymous client's pseudonym.
- ❌ No AI service that receives raw ciphertext server-side.
- ✅ MFA mandatory for Platform Admin and Tenant Admin.
- ✅ Yearly external security audit.
- ✅ E2EE for all chat content.
- ✅ Tenant isolation enforced at persistence layer.
