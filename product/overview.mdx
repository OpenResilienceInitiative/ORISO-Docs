## 1.1 What ORISO Is

ORISO is a **multi-tenant online counseling platform** that lets accredited counseling organizations offer text-based and live-chat counseling to clients **anonymously, GDPR-correctly, and at hospital-grade privacy**.

It is *not* a generic note-taking app. It is a regulated, privacy-first counseling product where:

- A **client** can ask for help without giving their real name.
- A **counselor** at a registered counseling center can pick up that request and chat in a secure, encrypted room.
- A **platform owner**, **tenant** (regional cluster of organizations), and **counselor admin** all live in a clean, isolated hierarchy so one organization can never see another's data.

Think of it as **"a hospital triage desk on the internet for counseling — but the patient never has to give their name, and the system is built to forget them as soon as they leave."**

## 1.2 Core Purpose

| Goal | What it means in product terms |
|---|---|
| **Anonymous help** | Clients enter with a generated pseudonym. No real identity is ever required. |
| **Regulated trust** | Every level (platform → tenant → counseling center) carries an enforceable Imprint, Data Policy Agreement, and GDPR Agreement. |
| **Real-time conversation** | A live-chat queue (waiting room → counselor pickup → consented chat) backed by Matrix Synapse. |
| **Hierarchical isolation** | One tenant cannot see another tenant's data. One counseling center cannot see another counseling center's clients. |
| **Disposable data** | If a client leaves the waiting room or closes the chat, their account and traces are wiped server-side within seconds to minutes. |

## 1.3 Target Users

### Primary users (people who use the app)

| User | Who they are in the real world | Why they use ORISO |
|---|---|---|
| **Client** | A person in distress (debt, family, addiction, health, etc.) seeking help | Wants quick, anonymous, no-registration counseling |
| **Counselor** | A trained counselor / advisor at a counseling center (e.g., Caritas Berlin) | Picks up tickets from the live-chat waiting room and runs sessions |
| **Counselor Admin** | The lead at a single counseling center | Manages counselors, configures the center's imprint/GDPR text, enables/disables live chat |
| **Tenant Admin** | Regional umbrella organization (e.g., "Caritas Germany") | Creates counseling centers under its tenant, signs the platform-tenant data policy agreement |
| **Platform Admin** | The ORISO platform operator | Onboards tenants, owns the master Imprint / GDPR / Data Policy templates, runs MFA-protected operations |

### Secondary users (people who reason about the system)

- **Developers** (frontend / backend) building ORISO and integrating with Keycloak, Matrix, LiveKit.
- **Designers** working on the live-chat onboarding, waiting-room and consent screens (see [UI/UX](/product/ui-ux)).
- **Product managers / business stakeholders** evaluating, pitching, or auditing the platform.
- **Security auditors** running yearly external audits (a stated requirement after the 2026 incident).

## 1.4 Key Problems It Solves

<CardGroup cols={2}>
<Card title="The 'I don't want to register' problem" icon="user-secret">
Most counseling tools force registration. ORISO lets a client enter the waiting room with just a generated pseudonym (e.g., *"geschmeidiges Kanninchen Kim"*) and a server-issued cookie — no email, no phone, no real name.
</Card>

<Card title="The GDPR-cascade problem" icon="scale-balanced">
Different organizations want different legal texts. ORISO uses **inheritance**: every counseling center inherits the Imprint, Data Policy and GDPR Agreement from its tenant (and ultimately from the platform), but can override them locally.
</Card>

<Card title="The multi-tenant isolation problem" icon="shield-halved">
Hospitals don't share patient lists. Tenants and counseling centers must not see each other. ORISO enforces this through Keycloak roles, tenant-scoped APIs, and per-center Matrix rooms.
</Card>

<Card title="The 'forget me' problem" icon="trash-can">
If a client gives up in the waiting room, their pseudonymous user must be **wiped**, not archived. ORISO's design rule is "delete fast, archive never" — auto-delete on disconnect, encrypted-only message storage, scheduled purge of stale rooms.
</Card>

<Card title="The pin/zip-code routing problem" icon="map-pin">
Different counselors serve different German postal-code regions and topics. ORISO routes inquiries by zip code (long term) and topic-attached live-chat links (short term) so a Berlin debt-counseling client lands on a Berlin debt counselor.
</Card>

<Card title="The 'live-chat off' UX problem" icon="toggle-on">
Hiding inactive features confuses counselors. ORISO instead shows the live-chat section in a **deactivated state** with a hint to enable it (so 95% of counselors who never use live chat aren't lost when the 5% turn it on).
</Card>
</CardGroup>

## 1.5 What ORISO Is Not

To prevent confusion, the product **does not**:

- Record audio/video of client sessions to build searchable transcripts (no AI note-taking; LiveKit handles WebRTC video, but recordings are out of scope).
- Persist client identity past the session (no "user history" for clients).
- Allow cross-tenant search, analytics, or reporting (isolation is enforced).
- Run any service in `dev` mode in production (a hard rule after the 2026 Keycloak incident).
